# Kerala Industrial Safety Regulations 2023

## Chapter 1: General Provisions

### Section 1.1: Scope and Application
These regulations apply to all industrial establishments in Kerala, including manufacturing units, processing plants, and assembly facilities. All new industrial units must obtain necessary clearances before commencing operations.

### Section 1.2: Definitions
- **Industrial Unit**: Any establishment engaged in manufacturing, processing, or assembly of goods
- **Competent Authority**: The designated officer responsible for enforcement
- **Hazardous Industry**: Industries dealing with flammable, toxic, or explosive materials

## Chapter 2: Fire Safety Requirements

### Section 2.1: Fire Extinguishers
All industrial units must maintain fire extinguishers at intervals not exceeding 15 meters. Fire extinguishers must be:
- Properly maintained and inspected quarterly
- Easily accessible and clearly marked
- Appropriate for the type of fire risk present

### Section 2.2: Emergency Exits
Every industrial building must have:
- Minimum two emergency exits on each floor
- Emergency exits clearly marked with illuminated signs
- Unobstructed access to all emergency exits at all times
- Emergency exit doors that open outward

### Section 2.3: Fire Safety Certificate
No industrial unit shall operate without a valid Fire Safety Certificate issued by the Fire Department. The certificate must be renewed annually.

## Chapter 3: Environmental Compliance

### Section 3.1: Air Pollution Control
Industries must install appropriate air pollution control equipment including:
- Electrostatic precipitators for particulate matter
- Scrubbers for gaseous emissions
- Regular monitoring of emissions

### Section 3.2: Water Management
Industries must:
- Obtain water source approval from the Water Authority
- Install water treatment facilities if using groundwater
- Maintain groundwater levels above 40 feet depth
- Implement rainwater harvesting systems

### Section 3.3: Waste Management
All industrial units must:
- Segregate waste into hazardous and non-hazardous categories
- Dispose of hazardous waste through authorized agencies only
- Maintain waste disposal records for minimum 5 years
- Submit quarterly waste management reports

## Chapter 4: Location and Buffer Zones

### Section 4.1: Residential Buffer Zone
Industrial units must maintain minimum distance from residential areas:
- Light industries: 100 meters
- Medium industries: 500 meters
- Heavy industries: 1000 meters
- Hazardous industries: 2000 meters

### Section 4.2: Water Body Protection
No industrial unit shall be established within 200 meters of any river, lake, or water body without special permission from the Environmental Authority.

## Chapter 5: Building and Safety Standards

### Section 5.1: Building Requirements
Industrial buildings must comply with:
- National Building Code standards
- Minimum ceiling height of 4 meters for manufacturing areas
- Adequate ventilation and lighting
- Separate storage areas for hazardous materials

### Section 5.2: Worker Safety
Industries must provide:
- Personal protective equipment to all workers
- First aid facilities on premises
- Safety training programs conducted quarterly
- Emergency response plan displayed prominently

## Chapter 6: Inspection and Compliance

### Section 6.1: Regular Inspections
All industrial units are subject to:
- Annual safety inspections by competent authorities
- Surprise inspections at any time
- Mandatory compliance with inspection findings within 30 days

### Section 6.2: Penalties
Non-compliance may result in:
- Warning notices for minor violations
- Fines ranging from ₹10,000 to ₹5,00,000
- Suspension of operations for serious violations
- Cancellation of license for repeated violations

## Chapter 7: Documentation Requirements

### Section 7.1: Mandatory Documents
Every industrial unit must maintain:
- Valid industrial license
- Fire safety certificate
- Environmental clearance certificate
- Building plan approval
- Water source NOC
- Waste disposal agreements
- Employee safety training records

### Section 7.2: Renewal Requirements
All certificates and licenses must be renewed:
- Industrial license: Every 5 years
- Fire safety certificate: Annually
- Environmental clearance: Every 3 years
- Building plan approval: One-time (unless modifications)
